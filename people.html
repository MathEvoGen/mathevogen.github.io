Himani Sachdeva.

I use mathematical models to understand how genetic variation in natural populations influences the short-term response to selection as well as longer-term evolutionary outcomes (local adaptation, speciation, extinction). My research focuses primarily on the evolution of complex (polygenic) traits, which often harbour high levels of variation. Selection on such traits typically results in subtle, non-independent changes across very many genes, which can be difficult to detect in genomic data. The goal of my reseach is to understand how the genomic signatures of such poygenic adaptation are influenced by the genetic architecture of selected traits (the numbers, effect sizes, linkage and pleiotropy of the underlying genes) as well as population processes, especially spatial population structure.

I studied physics and obtained my PhD in statistical physics from TIFR Mumbai. I then switched to theoretical population and evolutionary genetics for my postdoctoral work at IST Austria. I am currently a university assistant (senior postdoc) in the Mathematics and Biosciences Group at the University of Vienna. Beginning October 2025, I will be starting as an assistant professor with a joint appointment in the Faculty of Life Sciences and the Faculty of Mathematics at the University of Vienna. I am also a part of an SFB consortium on polygenic adaptation which consists of multiple groups combining different approaches --- experimental evolution, genomic analyses of natural populations and theoretical modeling --- to study polygenic adaptation.
