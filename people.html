Himani Sachdeva.
I use mathematical models to understand how genetic variation in natural populations influences short-term response to selection as well as longer-term evolutionary outcomes (local adaptation, speciation, extinction). My research mainly focuses on the evolution of complex (polygenic) traits, most of which harbour high levels of variation. Selection on such traits typically results in subtle, non-independent changes across very many genes, which can be difficult to detect in genomic data. I aim to understand how the genomic signatures of such poygenic adaptation are influenced by the genetic architecture of selected traits (the numbers, effect sizes, linkage and pleiotropy of the underlying genes) as well as population processes (including soatial populaiton strcuture). (see Research for more details).
I am currently a university assistant (senior postdoc) in the Mathematics and Biosciences Group at the University of Vienna. Beginning October 2025, I will be starting as an assistant professor with a joint appointment in the Faculty of Life Sciences and the Faculty of Mathematics at the University of Vienna. I am also a part of the SFB consortium which aims to undertstand polygenic adaptation using a combination of differen approaches -- experimental evolution, genomic analyses of natural population and theoretical modeling.
